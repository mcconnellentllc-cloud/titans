Main Dishes

Nashville Hot Chicken

The iconic Music City classic - bring the heat!

45 min Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken thighs & 4 drumsticks
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 tbsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp paprika, garlic powder, salt
  • 1/2 cup lard or bacon fat (for paste)
  • 3 tbsp cayenne (for hot paste)

Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken in buttermilk overnight in fridge.
  2. Mix flour with 1 tbsp cayenne, paprika, garlic, salt.
  3. Dredge chicken in flour mixture, shake off excess.
  4. Fry at 325°F for 15-18 minutes until golden and 165°F internal.
  5. Make hot paste: melt lard, add 3 tbsp cayenne, brown sugar, paprika.
  6. Brush hot paste generously over fried chicken.
  7. Serve on white bread with pickles. Titan Up!

Tennessee Smoked Brisket

Low and slow - worth every minute of the wait

10-12 hrs Serves 12

Ingredients

  • 12-14 lb whole packer brisket
  • 1/4 cup coarse black pepper
  • 1/4 cup coarse kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • Hickory or oak wood chunks
  • Apple cider vinegar spray
  • Butcher paper for wrapping

Instructions

  1. Trim brisket, leaving 1/4" fat cap.
  2. Apply rub generously, let sit 1 hour.
  3. Smoke at 225-250°F fat side up.
  4. Spritz with apple cider every hour after bark forms.
  5. Wrap in butcher paper at 165°F internal (the stall).
  6. Continue until 203°F and probe tender.
  7. Rest 1-2 hours before slicing against the grain.

Tennessee Pulled Pork

Smoky, tender, and perfect for sandwiches

8-10 hrs Serves 10

Ingredients

  • 8-10 lb bone-in pork shoulder (Boston butt)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp each: garlic, onion, cumin
  • 1 tbsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • Your favorite BBQ sauce

Instructions

  1. Mix all dry spices for the rub.
  2. Score fat cap, apply mustard as binder, then rub.
  3. Smoke at 225°F using apple or cherry wood.
  4. Spritz with apple cider vinegar hourly.
  5. Wrap in foil at 165°F with splash of apple juice.
  6. Cook until 205°F and bone pulls clean.
  7. Rest 30 min, then pull and mix with juices.

Southern Sides

Southern Mac & Cheese

Creamy, cheesy, and baked until golden with a crispy top. Use sharp cheddar, Velveeta for creaminess, and a touch of cream cheese.

Pro tip: Add a layer of breadcrumbs mixed with melted butter on top before baking.

Collard Greens

Slow-simmered with smoked ham hocks, onion, garlic, and a splash of apple cider vinegar. Season with red pepper flakes.

Pro tip: Cook low for 2-3 hours until silky tender. Save the pot liquor!

BBQ Baked Beans

Sweet and smoky with bacon, brown sugar, molasses, and your favorite BBQ sauce. Bake until thick and bubbly.

Pro tip: Add chopped burnt ends or pulled pork for extra meat!

Creamy Coleslaw

Crisp cabbage and carrots in a tangy mayo-based dressing with apple cider vinegar, sugar, and celery seed.

Pro tip: Make it a few hours ahead so flavors meld together.

Cast Iron Cornbread

Golden, slightly sweet cornbread baked in a screaming hot cast iron skillet with bacon drippings for crispy edges.

Pro tip: Preheat the skillet with butter/bacon fat before adding batter.

Crispy Fried Okra

Fresh okra sliced, dredged in seasoned cornmeal, and fried until golden and crunchy. Serve with ranch or comeback sauce.

Pro tip: Soak okra in buttermilk before breading for extra tender results.

Southern Potato Salad

Creamy with hard-boiled eggs, sweet pickle relish, celery, mustard, and mayo. Some families add a secret ingredient!

Pro tip: Season potatoes with salt while still warm so they absorb flavor.

Desserts

Southern Banana Pudding

Creamy, dreamy layers of pure comfort

30 min + chill Serves 10

Ingredients

  • 2 boxes vanilla wafers (Nilla)
  • 6 ripe bananas, sliced
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 box instant vanilla pudding
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 12 oz Cool Whip, thawed

Instructions

  1. Beat cream cheese until smooth.
  2. Add condensed milk, mix well.
  3. Add milk and pudding mix, beat until thick.
  4. Fold in Cool Whip gently.
  5. Layer: wafers, bananas, pudding. Repeat 3x.
  6. Top with wafers and refrigerate 4+ hours.

Tennessee Pecan Pie

Rich, gooey, and loaded with pecans

1 hr Serves 8

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked 9" pie crust
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup melted butter
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups pecan halves
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Whisk corn syrup, brown sugar, butter, eggs, vanilla, salt.
  3. Arrange pecans in pie crust.
  4. Pour filling over pecans.
  5. Bake 50-55 minutes until set (slight jiggle in center).
  6. Cool completely before slicing.

Southern Peach Cobbler

Warm, sweet peaches with buttery biscuit topping

50 min Serves 8

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fresh peaches (or 2 cans, drained)
  • 1 cup sugar (divided)
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 stick butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Vanilla ice cream for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Pour melted butter in 9x13 baking dish.
  3. Mix flour, 1/2 cup sugar, baking powder, milk into batter.
  4. Pour batter over butter (don't stir!).
  5. Toss peaches with remaining sugar and cinnamon.
  6. Spoon peaches over batter (still don't stir!).
  7. Bake 45-50 min until golden. Serve warm with ice cream.

Tennessee Chess Pie

A Southern classic - simple but irresistible

55 min Serves 8

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked 9" pie crust
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tbsp cornmeal
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F.
  2. Whisk sugar and melted butter.
  3. Beat in eggs one at a time.
  4. Add cornmeal, vinegar, vanilla, salt.
  5. Pour into pie crust.
  6. Bake 50-55 minutes until set with slight wobble.
  7. Cool completely - filling will set as it cools.

Goo Goo Cluster Brownies

Inspired by Nashville's famous candy since 1912

45 min Makes 16

Ingredients

  • 1 box brownie mix + ingredients
  • 1 cup marshmallow fluff
  • 1 cup caramel sauce
  • 1 cup roasted peanuts, chopped
  • 1 cup milk chocolate chips
  • 2 tbsp butter

Instructions

  1. Bake brownies per package in 9x13 pan. Cool 10 min.
  2. Spread marshmallow fluff over warm brownies.
  3. Drizzle caramel sauce over fluff.
  4. Sprinkle with chopped peanuts.
  5. Melt chocolate chips with butter, drizzle on top.
  6. Refrigerate 1 hour before cutting.

Game Day Drinks

Southern Sweet Tea

The house wine of the South

Mix: 4 family-size tea bags, 1 cup sugar, 1 gallon water. Brew tea in 4 cups boiling water 5 min. Add sugar while hot, stir until dissolved. Add remaining cold water. Serve over ice with lemon.

Lynchburg Lemonade

Jack Daniel's signature cocktail

Mix: 1.5 oz Jack Daniel's, 1 oz triple sec, 1 oz fresh lemon juice, top with lemon-lime soda. Serve over ice in a tall glass with lemon wheel garnish.

Tennessee Whiskey Sour

Smooth Tennessee whiskey meets citrus

Mix: 2 oz Tennessee whiskey, 1 oz fresh lemon juice, 3/4 oz simple syrup, dash of bitters. Shake with ice, strain into rocks glass. Garnish with cherry and orange.

Titans Blue Margarita

Team colors in a glass - Titan Up!

Mix: 2 oz tequila, 1 oz blue curaçao, 1 oz fresh lime juice, 1/2 oz agave. Shake with ice, strain into salt-rimmed glass. Perfect for game day!

Nashville Bushwacker

Frozen boozy milkshake - Broadway favorite

Blend: 1 oz each: Kahlua, dark rum, amaretto, dark crème de cacao. Add 2 oz cream of coconut, 2 oz half & half, 1 cup ice. Blend until smooth. Top with whipped cream.

Bourbon Slush

Make-ahead frozen punch perfection

Mix & Freeze: 2 cups bourbon, 1 can frozen lemonade, 1 can frozen OJ, 6 cups water, 1 cup sugar, 2 tea bags steeped. Freeze overnight. Scrape into glasses, top with lemon-lime soda.

Spiked Arnold Palmer

The classic combo with a Tennessee twist

Mix: 2 oz sweet tea vodka (or regular vodka), 4 oz fresh lemonade, 4 oz unsweetened iced tea. Stir over ice in tall glass. Garnish with lemon wedge and mint sprig.

Tailgate Pro Tips

1

Prep ahead: Make rubs, marinades, and side dishes the night before. Morning-of is for cooking meat!

2

Temperature matters: Invest in a good meat thermometer. Brisket at 203°F, chicken at 165°F, pork at 205°F.

3

Rest your meat: Let brisket and pork rest wrapped for at least 30 minutes. The juices redistribute!

4

Keep it hot: Use insulated coolers with towels to keep smoked meats warm for hours.

5

Batch your drinks: Make a big batch of cocktails in a dispenser so you can enjoy the game!

6

Don't forget the basics: Napkins, wet wipes, trash bags, and hand sanitizer are tailgate essentials.
