Franchise Timeline

1960

The Houston Oilers Are Born

The franchise began as the Houston Oilers, one of the original eight teams in the American Football League (AFL). Owner Bud Adams built a team that would become a charter member of the new league.

The Oilers won the first two AFL Championships in 1960 and 1961, establishing themselves as an early dynasty in professional football.

1960-1961

Back-to-Back AFL Champions

Led by quarterback George Blanda, the Oilers captured consecutive AFL titles. Blanda's versatility as both quarterback and kicker made him one of the most valuable players in the league.

1978-1980

The Luv Ya Blue Era

Coach Bum Phillips led the Oilers to three consecutive playoff appearances. The team featured Earl Campbell, who won three consecutive NFL rushing titles and the 1979 MVP award. The "Luv Ya Blue" rallying cry united Houston fans in unprecedented ways.

1987-1993

The Run-and-Shoot Era

The Oilers adopted the innovative Run-and-Shoot offense under coach Jerry Glanville and later Jack Pardee. Warren Moon emerged as one of the NFL's premier quarterbacks, throwing for over 4,000 yards in four consecutive seasons.

1997

The Move to Tennessee

After years of stadium disputes in Houston, owner Bud Adams relocated the franchise to Tennessee. The team played as the Tennessee Oilers for two seasons while their permanent stadium was being constructed.

1999

The Tennessee Titans Are Born

The franchise officially became the Tennessee Titans, debuting in their new stadium (now Nissan Stadium) in Nashville. The new identity represented Greek mythology's Titans - powerful beings that ruled before the Olympian gods.

January 8, 2000

The Music City Miracle

In one of the most dramatic plays in NFL history, Frank Wycheck threw a lateral to Kevin Dyson, who raced 75 yards for a touchdown with just 3 seconds remaining to defeat the Buffalo Bills 22-16 in the AFC Wild Card game.

The play, officially called "Home Run Throwback," remains one of the most replayed moments in football history and cemented the Titans' place in NFL lore.

January 30, 2000

Super Bowl XXXIV - One Yard Short

The Titans faced the St. Louis Rams in Super Bowl XXXIV in Atlanta. In a thrilling finale, the Titans drove down the field in the final minutes, trailing 23-16.

On the final play, Steve McNair connected with Kevin Dyson at the goal line, but Mike Jones made the tackle at the one-yard line as time expired. The Rams won 23-16, leaving the Titans heartbreakingly close to a championship.

2003

McNair Wins Co-MVP

Steve McNair shared the NFL MVP award with Peyton Manning, becoming the first Titan to win the honor. McNair played through numerous injuries to lead the team to a 12-4 record and the AFC Championship game.

2008

13-3 Season

Under coach Jeff Fisher, the Titans posted a 13-3 record and earned the #1 seed in the AFC. Running back Chris Johnson and quarterback Kerry Collins led a balanced offense that dominated opponents throughout the regular season.

2009

CJ2K

Chris Johnson rushed for 2,006 yards, becoming only the sixth player in NFL history to break the 2,000-yard barrier. His incredible speed earned him the nickname "CJ2K" and made him one of the most exciting players in the league.

2019-2020

Derrick Henry's Dominance

Running back Derrick Henry emerged as the most dominant force in football. In the 2019 playoffs, Henry rushed for 195 yards against the New England Patriots and 195 yards against the Baltimore Ravens, leading upset victories over both teams.

Henry won back-to-back rushing titles in 2019 and 2020, including a 2,027-yard season that marked only the eighth 2,000-yard rushing season in NFL history.

2021

#1 Seed in the AFC

Despite losing Derrick Henry to injury midseason, the Titans secured the #1 seed in the AFC with a 12-5 record. The team showed remarkable resilience, using a league-high 91 players due to injuries throughout the season.

Present

Building for the Future

The Tennessee Titans continue to build toward their ultimate goal - bringing a Super Bowl championship to Nashville. With a passionate fanbase and a proud history, the franchise looks toward a bright future.
