Remember the Titans

The Inspirational Classic That Captures the Titans Spirit

2000 - Denzel Washington

About the Film

2000 PG 1h 53m Drama/Sport Based on True Story

Remember the Titans tells the true story of the 1971 T.C. Williams High School football team in Alexandria, Virginia. When the school is integrated and African American coach Herman Boone (Denzel Washington) is appointed head coach over the beloved white coach Bill Yoast, the team must overcome racial tensions to become state champions.

While not about the NFL Tennessee Titans, this film embodies everything we love about being Titans fans - overcoming adversity, building unity, and rising to greatness against all odds. Both teams share the name and the fighting spirit. Titan Up!

Did You Know?

  • The real T.C. Williams Titans went 13-0 in 1971 and won the Virginia AAA State Championship.
  • Denzel Washington trained with real football coaches to authentically portray Coach Boone.
  • The film was shot in Georgia, not Virginia, due to production logistics.
  • The real Gerry Bertier and Julius Campbell remained close friends until Bertier's death in 1981.
  • The movie grossed over $136 million worldwide against a $30 million budget.
  • It holds a 93% audience score on Rotten Tomatoes - a true fan favorite!
  • The "Titans" name was chosen by T.C. Williams in 1965 - 34 years before our NFL team became the Tennessee Titans!
